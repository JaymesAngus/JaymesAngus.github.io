The Statistics of Microlensing Photometry


is the name of my final year dissertation for my MPhys degree.

The animation on my homepage shows the model light curve for a simple gravitational microlensing event (solid line) followed by the simulated results of a ground-based telescope observation of the event (scatter plot). The blip on the right wing of the light curve is a planetary perturbation caused by the presence of a Jupiter analogue.

This simulation is currently oversimplified as I have not yet implemented correlated noise into my model, but it has produced some interesting preliminary results!
